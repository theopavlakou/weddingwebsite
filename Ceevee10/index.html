Information for non-Cypriots

Now that you've let us know that you'll be joining us, you can start planning yourself a holiday on this beautiful island!

Flights

Though the island is small, it's very popular (especially among the British), so make sure you book your flights nice and early! We advise you to go onto Scyscanner and set up a price alert so that you know when flights to Cyprus are at a reasonable price.

The best airport to go to would be Larnaca airport, as this is both closest to the venue and the area we recommend most of our guests to stay.

The airlines we recommend are British Airways (classy but usually expensive), Cyprus Airways (second to British Airways, but still a little bit hard on the wallet) and EasyJet (Ahhh, the student life). Ryanair also have a flight but it's to Paphos which means about an hour and fifteen minute journey to Larnaca, which isn't that great after a 5 hour flight.

Accommodation

Though the wedding itself will take place in Larnaca, we recommend that our guests stay either in Ayia Napa (30 minutes away) or Protaras (40 minutes away). These two places boast some of the best beaches in Cyprus and even Europe.

We recommend either using a website like Trivago (yes the one with the annoying adverts on the Tube) to book a hotel or alternatively renting a house for the week and sharing with a few people. There are a few websites which could help with your search. These include Holiday Lettings, Owners Direct, Home Away, etc. Though these don't include meals, they could turn out to be cheapern with the added comfort of being in a home and having a private swimming pool.

Take a scuba diving lesson

Get a group together and take a scuba diving trial lesson. We recommend Nemo. The instructor, Alexandros, is a great guy and makes the experience absolutely worth while. The bigger the group, the better the discount also :)

Emerge yourself into the ancient world

Cyprus has a rich history. Being a central link between Europe, the Middle East and Africa, Cyprus has always been coveted by all the great empires. Find out more here.

Ride a camel

Get a taste of the how Cypriots used to travel before cars were invented (okay, that's a lie, but seriously it's not far from the truth) and get yourself on a camel at the Camel Park in Mazotos.

Get an adrenaline rush at the water park

Cyprus has two water parks: one in Ayia Napa, Water World, which is the largest themed water park in Europe (what that means: I have no idea), and one in Limassol, Fasouri. Both are great but some people prefer one over the other. One thing to note is that Fasouri is significantly cheaper than Water World having an admission price of €29 for adults and €16 for children as opposed to €36 and €22 respectively.

Cool down up in the mountains

For those who enjoy forestry more than the sea, Cyprus has just the thing for you. The largest mountain range is called Troodos and it's peak is at roughly 2,000 meters above sea level. It offers a cool escape from the heat, which could reach in the high 30's (Celsius) in Larnaca and Ayia Napa, being in the mid 20's on average in the summer. The tranquility allows you to just lay back, breathe some mountain air and just take in the beauty of God's creation.

Warnings about Cyprus

Driving

Cyprus has to have the worst drivers in Europe (and must rank quite high in the world as well), so be prepared! Just pretend that everyone is intentionally trying to crash into you and you should be fine.
